Why CCL calls all teams, a "Tribe"?

A compelling choice for unity and purpose.

CCL believes that referring to our team as a tribe, this somehow creates a strong sense of belonging among team members. It suggests that they are part of a close-knit group with shared values, goals, and a deep connection.

Tribes historically represent groups that stick together through thick and thin. This term can symbolize the idea that our team will work together and support one another, just like a tribe does.

Tribes often have their own unique cultures and traditions. By adopting this concept, CCL encourages our team to develop its own distinctive culture, rituals, and ways of working that foster a strong sense of identity.

Tribes often unite for a common purpose, such as survival or achieving a specific goal. By framing our team as a tribe, CCL certainly emphasizes the importance of a shared mission and the need for everyone to work together to achieve it.

In a tribe, cooperation and collaboration are essential for survival. By using this term in our team, CCL highlights the importance of working together harmoniously to overcome challenges and achieve success.

The concept of a tribe often implies that everyone is equal and has a role to play. This can promote inclusivity within our team, ensuring that all members feel valued and contribute to the group's success.

In many tribes, each member has specific responsibilities. By using this concept, CCL encourages team members to take ownership of their roles and be accountable for their contributions.

Tribes are known for their adaptability in the face of changing circumstances. This can be a valuable trait for your team, emphasizing the need to be flexible and responsive to evolving challenges and opportunities.

Tribes often empower individual members to make decisions for the benefit of the group. This can encourage team members to take initiative and be proactive in solving problems.

The idea of a tribe can be motivating, as it taps into primal instincts of cooperation and working together for a common cause. It can inspire team members to give their best effort.

When using the concept of a tribe to unite our team, CCL ensures that it's used in a way that is respectful and culturally sensitive. Additionally, CCL is always clear about our team's mission, values, and goals to ensure that everyone understands the shared purpose they are working towards as a tribe.
